Ash
The blockchain enables you to use a trustless model, which doesn't make sense in any traditional game, virtual world where you're running servers by the company and the company pretty much controls all inputs and outputs anyway.

A decentralized server system, such as Neos, where anyone can run a server currently has a single authority that can basically manipulate all the data and assets right now. What blockchain allows us to do is to basically stop that trust on a single authority and instead move that trust into cryptographic functions that, if designed correctly, cannot be manipulated by a single central authority.

I haven't discussed storage of assets, because it's not an interesting conversation. It doesn't have to be in the blockchain, but it will need tight integration in some way to work well and done in a way to prevent a 3rd party from successfully tampering and manipulating the asset.

When discussing issues of dupes and such, these are just regular problems that are inherently resolved in the blockchain due to how it operates, I am not saying they can't be resolved without the blockchain though.

Further, the other problems that blockchain solves on a trustless system can be trustless clarity about what will happen when you take action; because of functionality like smart contracts (or specific protocol commands). The only thing you have to trust at that point is what it actually says it will do, and what it will do if it fails, rather than relying on a 3rd party who may seize, delete, manipulate etc.

The one thing you haven't mentioned that is a problem that needs to be considered, is how to address country specific laws that say no hosting of certain content due to takedown orders, copyright infringement etc. I think there is where blockhain has a weakness; but, even there, leaving it up to the individual server host to blacklist or even share blacklisted content with other server hosts in the same country/countries/friends is probably an adequate option here.
